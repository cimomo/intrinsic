## 2026-04-04 — MSFT + GOOGL analysis

### Cost of debt calculation produces implausible values

**Found during:** GOOGL calibrate (auto mode)

Google's interest expense / total debt = 0.3%. This dragged WACC down and distorted the reverse DCF (market-implied growth showed 5.2% which is nonsensically low for an 18%-growing company). The sanity check in the skill flagged it correctly ("cost of debt well below risk-free rate"), but I had to manually decide to use 3.5% instead.

**The problem:** Many large tech companies have minimal interest expense relative to debt (legacy bonds at low coupons, significant interest income offsetting). The simple interest/debt calculation produces numbers that break WACC.

**Suggested fix:** When cost of debt < risk-free rate - 100bps, auto-adjust to risk-free rate - 50bps (or similar heuristic) rather than just warning. The current behavior requires manual judgment during auto mode, which defeats the purpose of auto.

**Files:** `skills/calibrate/SKILL.md` (mechanical assumptions section), possibly `stock_analyzer/dcf.py`

### Confidence-adjusted recommendation bands are too blunt

**Found during:** MSFT value

The "downgrade one level" mechanism for Medium confidence produces edge-case issues. At 3.2% upside, standard bands give HOLD. Medium confidence downgrades one level — but HOLD downgraded is SELL, which seems wrong for 3.2% upside. The mechanism happened to be a no-op for MSFT (HOLD stays HOLD regardless), but the underlying logic is flawed.

**Suggested fix:** Instead of shifting all bands down, widen the HOLD band when confidence is lower. E.g., Medium confidence: HOLD range becomes -15% to +15% (instead of -10% to +10%). Low confidence: HOLD range becomes -20% to +20%. This avoids the "3% upside = SELL" problem.

**Files:** `skills/value/SKILL.md` (recommendation section)

### Near-zero FCF in years 1-5 should trigger stronger warning

**Found during:** GOOGL value

GOOGL's DCF produced $6-7B annual FCF in years 1-5 on a $3.6T company. That's essentially zero — the entire valuation lives in years 6-10 and terminal value (84.7% of EV). The sanity check flagged terminal value concentration, but didn't connect it to the near-zero early FCF being the CAUSE.

**Suggested fix:** When years 1-5 FCF sum is <5% of enterprise value, add explicit warning: "Near-term cash flows contribute almost nothing to the valuation. The BUY recommendation depends entirely on years 6-10 assumptions and terminal value. Consider whether you're comfortable with this level of long-term dependency."

**Files:** `skills/value/SKILL.md` (sanity check section)

### Auto mode produces internally consistent but untested assumptions

**Found during:** GOOGL full pipeline (--auto)

Auto mode for GOOGL produced zero alignment disconnects and a clean BUY. But nobody challenged the 0.55 S/C ratio or asked "why not 0.65?" The MSFT interactive session produced a better analysis because the user pushed back on assumptions, asked for historical data, and we landed on more thoughtful numbers together.

**Not a bug — a design observation.** Auto mode is working as designed. But the report should note when all assumptions were auto-derived: "All assumptions are auto-derived — no manual overrides. Consider running /calibrate interactively to stress-test the assumptions you're least sure about."

**Files:** `skills/report/SKILL.md` (verdict or alignment check section)

### FCF yield warning for CapEx-heavy companies needs forward estimate

**Found during:** GOOGL value

Trailing FCF yield was 4.3% for GOOGL, which sounds healthy. But with 2026 CapEx doubling to $180B, forward FCF could be near zero. The skill noted this but didn't calculate a forward estimate. Should compute: Revenue × (1+g) × margin × (1-tax) - (Revenue × g / S/C) = approximate forward FCF.

**Files:** `skills/value/SKILL.md` (step 2, FCF yield section)

### Reverse DCF sensitivity to held-constant assumptions

**Found during:** GOOGL calibrate

The reverse DCF for GOOGL showed 5.2% implied growth with the problematic 0.3% cost of debt, then 13.3% after adjusting to 3.5%. A 3.2% change in cost of debt swung implied growth by 8 percentage points. The reverse DCF is sensitive to its held-constant assumptions in ways that aren't obvious.

**Suggested fix:** Run reverse DCF twice with slightly different held-constant assumptions (e.g., ±1% on margin) and show the range: "Market-implied growth: 11-15% depending on margin assumption." A point estimate overstates precision.

**Files:** `skills/calibrate/SKILL.md` (reverse DCF section), possibly `stock_analyzer/dcf.py` (add range parameter to reverse_dcf)
